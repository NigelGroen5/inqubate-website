# Current Projects

## Paperboy

**Type:** Mobile App

Paperboy is a mobile app that transforms the newsletters you receive each morning into a single, personalized audio briefing. Instead of reading through a crowded inbox, users can simply press play and stay informed during their commute or morning routine. By turning high-quality written content into a calm, daily listening experience, Paperboy helps users feel caught up without the friction of scrolling or reading.

## Orbit

**Type:** Web Platform

Lost and found systems today are fragmented, inefficient, and largely broken. At Queen's alone, students must check 20+ locations to recover a single item while lost and found bins overflow with unclaimed valuables worth thousands of dollars. Orbiit is building a centralized digital platform to fix this. Users can upload lost or found items with location data or drop them at the nearest partnered hub. On the other side, users searching for items submit a description, and a matching algorithm connects them to the most likely results — eliminating the need to search blindly across campus. Built by Queen's students for Queen's students, Orbiit is launching its MVP in Kingston with a vision to scale across Canadian universities — modernizing lost and found for a digital-first world.
